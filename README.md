Description of the task :

This task involves solving a business optimization problem where a company must decide how to allocate its limited resources to maximize profit. The focus is on determining the best combination of two products—Product A and Product B—that should be produced given certain constraints. This type of problem is commonly addressed using Linear Programming, a mathematical approach used to find the best possible outcome under a set of limitations.

The company produces two products, each with a different profit contribution and resource requirement. Product A yields a profit of $20 per unit, while Product B yields a higher profit of $30 per unit. However, producing these products requires resources such as labor and raw materials, both of which are limited. Product A requires 2 hours of labor and 3 kg of material per unit, whereas Product B requires 4 hours of labor and 2 kg of material per unit. The company has a maximum of 100 labor hours and 60 kg of material available.

The task begins by defining decision variables to represent the number of units of each product to produce. Let x represent Product A and y represent Product B. The next step is to construct an objective function, which represents the total profit: maximize 20x + 30y. This function is what the company aims to optimize.

Alongside the objective function, constraints are defined to represent the limitations on resources. The labor constraint ensures that total labor used does not exceed available hours (2x + 4y ≤ 100), while the material constraint ensures that total material usage stays within the available supply (3x + 2y ≤ 60). Additionally, non-negativity constraints ensure that production quantities cannot be negative.

Using tools such as Python and optimization libraries, this mathematical model can be solved to find the optimal production mix. The solution provides the values of x and y that maximize profit without violating any constraints.

This task is important because it demonstrates how mathematical models can be applied to real-world business problems. It helps in making informed decisions about resource allocation, production planning, and cost efficiency. By analyzing the results, businesses can identify which products are more profitable and how to utilize resources effectively.

Overall, the task highlights the practical application of linear programming in operations management. It shows how businesses can use data-driven approaches to improve productivity, reduce waste, and maximize profits. The same approach can be extended to more complex scenarios involving multiple products, additional constraints, and larger-scale industrial systems.
